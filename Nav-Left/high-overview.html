History & exam

Key factors

  • presence of risk factors
  • acute onset
  • urticaria
  • angio-oedema
  • flushing
  • dyspnoea
  • wheezing
  • rhinitis

Other diagnostic factors

  • allergen ingestion
  • insect stings or bites
  • pruritus
  • inspiratory stridor and hoarse voice
  • bilateral conjunctivitis
  • nausea and vomiting
  • abdominal cramping or pain
  • agitation/anxiety
  • confusion/disorientation
  • tachycardia
  • dizziness
  • syncope
  • sense of impending doom (angor animi)
  • recent exercise
  • hot or cold exposure
  • hypotension
  • bradycardia
  • cardiac arrest
  • diarrhoea
  • visual disturbances
  • tremor
  • seizures

Diagnostic tests

1st tests to order

  • serum tryptase level

Tests to consider

  • in vitro IgE testing
  • skin test
  • challenge test

Emerging tests

  • basophil activation test

Treatment details

Presumptive

all patients: acute onset

    • 1st line: cardiorespiratory assessment + supportive measures
    • plus: IM epinephrine (adrenaline)
    • plus: assess & secure airway
    • plus: IV normal saline
  • cardiopulmonary arrest
    • plus: CPR + IV epinephrine (adrenaline)
  • severe hypotension
    • plus: IV epinephrine (adrenaline)
    • adjunct: IV glucagon
  • persistent respiratory symptoms
    • plus: inhaled beta-2 agonist
  • symptomatic hives and rhinorrhoea
    • adjunct: antihistamine (H1 antagonist) + H2 antagonist
  • post-emergency stabilisation
    • adjunct: corticosteroids

Ongoing

patients with identified allergen

    • 1st line: immunotherapy
