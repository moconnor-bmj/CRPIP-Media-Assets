History & exam

Key factors

  • family history of sudden cardiac arrest
  • valvular heart disease
  • pulmonary disease
  • medicines which prolong the QT interval or cause electrolyte disturbances
  • illicit substances
  • trauma
  • chest pain
  • palpitations
  • syncope
  • presence of other risk factors

Other diagnostic factors

  • hypertension
  • hypercholesterolaemia
  • diabetes mellitus
  • kidney disease
  • family history of coronary artery disease
  • elevated jugular venous pulse
  • heart murmur
  • abnormalities on auscultation of lungs
  • tracheal deviation
  • neurological deficits
  • history of eating disorders

History & exam details

Diagnostic tests

1st tests to order

  • ECG
  • FBC
  • serum electrolytes
  • ABG
  • cardiac biomarkers
  • toxicology screen
  • CXR
  • echocardiogram
  • coronary angiography

Tests to consider

  • cardiac magnetic resonance imaging
  • signal-averaged electrocardiogram (SAECG)
  • electrophysiological study

Diagnostic tests details

Treatment details

Presumptive

unwitnessed cardiac arrest

    • 1st line: CPR

Acute

shockable rhythms (pulseless ventricular tachycardia or ventricular fibrillation)

    • 1st line: CPR and defibrillation
    • plus: vasopressor
    • adjunct: magnesium
    • adjunct: anti-arrhythmic

non-shockable rhythms (pulseless electrical activity or asystole)

    • 1st line: CPR and vasopressor
    • plus: atropine

Ongoing

return of spontaneous circulation

    • 1st line: post-resuscitation care

no return of spontaneous circulation

    • 1st line: continue or consider termination of resuscitation
